My Journey to Tech

I grew up in the 80's. I didn't have a computer until my early 20's.

When I was in high school I quit typing class and decided I was going into the medical field. I figured I wouldn't have to learn typing. I was dead wrong, even though it took several years the medical field is now full of electronic charting.

After spending 20 years in the medical field and raising my three children, I couldn't help but notice all the cool careers that are out in the world today. I am still young enough to want to learn and try a new career. I tried different online businesses with some success, but I wasn't into it, until I found tech.

I love the ability to use my creativity, problem solving skills, learning new things and applying what I learn.

This field satisfies just that for me. I enjoy the flexibility so I can still work in the medical field and do freelance tech or remote tech jobs. Who said you can't have your cake and eat it too?
